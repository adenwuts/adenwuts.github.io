EEG

Advantages

EEG is a direct measure of electrical brain activity, with high temporal resolution and precision that can match the speed of cognition. EEG also contains a lot of potential information within the data, allowing many for kinds of analysis.

Disadvantages

EEG is "limited to large-scale potentials", meaning we can't measure the activity of single or even a few neurons. Many brain actions take place at a spatial scale too small to be measured or analysed effectively by EEG.

Some signals can also cancel eachother out (i.e. positive right next to negative), meaning they're hidden from EEG.

It can also be difficult to localise the data to a physical anatomical location, since we only measure from the outside of the skull.
